Shigeru Miyamoto

Shigeru Miyamoto is a video game designer, producer, and director at Nintendo, as well as one of that company's managing directors. He is the creator of numerous highly regarded game franchies including Mario, The Legend of Zelda, Star Fox, and Donkey Kong. To list every notable title Miyamoto has worked on would take far too much space. The most important games he has ever been involved with have been genre-defining: Super Mario Bros. (Famicom, 1985), The Legend of Zelda (Famicom Disk System, 1986), and Donkey Kong (arcade, 1981).

Miyamoto was born in Sonobe, Japan, in 1952 and began working at Nintendo in 1977. Before Miyamoto entered the company, Nintendo was a fairly obscure Japanese company that made playing cards and toys, though the company was beginning to enter the video game market by the time Miyamoto came around. Donkey Kong, originally intended to be based upon the Popeye franchise, was released in 1981 and became an immediate hit around the world.

Over the next 40 years, Miyamoto would contribute to dozens of games for every home and handheld console Nintendo made. The iconic design of the Super Famicom's controller is Miyamoto's work, as an example. Even on franchises where Miyamoto did not contribute directly, he mentored others within Nintendo.
